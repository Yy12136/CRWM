Abstract

The capability of imagining internally with a mental model of the world is important for intelligent agents. However, existing Large Language Model (LLM)-driven paradigms for Automated Reward Design (ARD) fundamentally lack such causal understanding. Relying on observational correlations embedded in physical trajectories without decoupling confounding factors, these methods suffer from profound mechanistic blindness. Consequently, they often misinterpret spurious correlates as true causal prerequisites, triggering systemic bottlenecks such as \textit{Optimization Collapse} and \textit{Specification Gaming}, which necessitate computationally prohibitive evolutionary trial-and-error loops. In this paper, we propose Causal Reward World Models (CRWMs) to enable unconfounded causal structural reasoning. Through a joint optimization engine, our framework distills a pure, task-invariant causal skeleton from continuous multi-task dynamics by explicitly decoupling state-dependent transient interactions. Elevating this refined structural prior empowers the LLM to execute rigorous counterfactual reasoning and causal pruning during the synthesis phase—structurally allocating zero weight to spurious correlates. Extensive experiments across complex continuous control tasks demonstrate that CRWM effectively guides the eradication of non-causal vulnerabilities responsible for these failures, achieving true zero-shot reward generation that not only exhibits exceptional efficiency but also yields task performance that closely approaches state-of-the-art (SOTA) results.

Framework

Overview of the Causal Reward World Model (CRWM) framework The pipeline consists of three synergistic phases: (1) \textbf{Structural Prior Extraction:} Prior interventional data ($\mathcal{D}_{off}$) is bifurcated into macroscopic ($\mathcal{D}_{macro}$) and microscopic ($\mathcal{D}_{micro}$) streams. A pre-trained causal foundation model (LimiX) processes $\mathcal{D}_{macro}$ to extract the initial, coarse structural prior $\langle M_{prior}, M_{conf} \rangle$. (2) \textbf{Joint Optimization Engine:} The Explicit Mechanism Decoupling (EMD) module separates environment dynamics into an invariant causal skeleton $M_{inv}$ (blue stream) and transient interactions $M_{trans}$ (orange stream) to form the instantaneous graph $M_{inst}$. Subsequently, the joint optimization engine utilizes $\mathcal{D}_{micro}$ to continuously optimize this structure via Confidence-Aware Soft Fusion, thereby distilling the final CRWM ($M_{inv}^*$). (3) \textbf{Causal-ARD:} The distilled causal topology, combined with unseen task information, is deployed within our Causal-ARD framework to guide the downstream Large Language Model (LLM). Through explicit causal pruning, the LLM synthesizes executable zero-shot reward functions.
